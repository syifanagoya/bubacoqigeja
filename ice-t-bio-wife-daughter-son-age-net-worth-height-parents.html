The hip-hop music industry has been graced with the exceptional talent of various rappers, who in turn have become standards by which others are inspired and measured. Many of them, like Ice-T, come from very rough backgrounds and against all odds have become phenomenal icons recognized worldwide.

Ice-T has managed to carve out his own brand of lyric that paints a picture of street life and violence, things that have heavily shaped and influenced the gangster rap genre. He has since pursued an acting career, appearing as a recurring cast member on the series Law & Order: Task Force Victims since 2000.

Ice-T Bio (age)

Ice-T was born on February 16, 1958 in Newark, New Jersey. He lost his parents at a young age, which meant he had to live with relatives. He first stayed with a nearby aunt before moving to View Park-Windsor Hills, an upper-middle-class black neighborhood in South Los Angeles to stay with another relative. He shared a room with his cousin Earl, who was a fan of rock music. It is said that Earl’s constant listening to local rock radio stations sparked Ice-T’s interest in heavy metal music.

Ice attended Palms Junior High and then Crenshaw High School. Despite the large presence of gangs in his high school, the teenager did not smoke, use drugs, drink alcohol, and was not a gang member. However, he was later linked to the Crips.

Tracy enjoyed reading Iceberg Slim’s novels, which he memorized and recited to his friends. The story goes that his friends enjoyed hearing the excerpts and said to him, “Yo, kick some more of that through Ice, T,” giving him his popular nickname.

career

Tracy began his career as a disc jockey and took the Ice-T name as a tribute to Iceberg Slim. While performing as a DJ, he received more recognition for his rap skills and decided to pursue a rap career. In 1987 he released his debut album Rhyme Pays , which was certified Gold by the RIAA. His second album, The Iceberg / Free Speech… Watch What You Say , was released in 1988 and earned him his second gold record and rave reviews.

In 1991 the OG Original Gangster appeared ; an album that defined gangster rap. On the album, he introduced his heavy metal band Body Count on a track of the same name. Their first album, Counting the Dead was released in March 1992. Through his own label, Rhyme Syndicate, Ice-T has released solo and band albums, the most recent being 2017’s Body Count album Blutrausch .

He made his film debut in the 1984 film Breaking In And Its 1985 Sequel Breakin’ 2: Electric Boogaloo . Over the years, Ice-T has also acted in films alongside Denzel Washington and John Lithgow.

Coco eventually created her own quarterly magazine called “R-Rated” and appeared in low budget films, TV series, talk shows and reality shows. Her most notable appearances are on Law & Order: Task Force Victims , Celebrity Family Feud , and Ice Cream Loves Coco.

parents

Ice-T’s was born to an African American father named Solomon and a Creole mother named Alice Marrow. It is not clear what his mother did for a living, but his father worked for decades as a conveyor mechanic at the Rapistan Conveyor Company.

When Ice-T was in third grade, his mother died of a heart attack. He was then raised by his father, who later died of a heart attack at the age of 12.
